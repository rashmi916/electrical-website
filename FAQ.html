Electrical F.A.Q.

If You Have a Question, RR Electricals Has the Answer!

How can I save money on my electric bill? +

You can reduce bills by using LED bulbs, turning off appliances, and checking wiring regularly.

How do I reset a ‘tripped’ circuit breaker? +

Locate the breaker panel and switch the tripped breaker to OFF then back to ON.

What is a GFI? +

A GFI protects you from shocks by cutting power if it detects imbalance.

Is it safe to use multiple extension cords? +

No. Using too many extensions can cause overheating and fire hazards.

Why do my lights flicker? +

Lights may flicker due to loose wiring, overloaded circuits, or a failing bulb or switch.

Why does my circuit breaker keep tripping? +

It trips when a circuit is overloaded, shorted, or has a faulty appliance connected.

How often should I have my home electrical system checked? +

It’s recommended to have a professional inspection every 3–5 years.

Why is my electricity bill suddenly high? +

Possible reasons include faulty wiring, new appliances, or high-power devices running longer.

What appliances use the most electricity at home? +

AC units, water heaters, refrigerators, and washing machines consume the most power.

Why does my fan run slow? +

This may be due to low voltage, capacitor damage, or dust inside the motor.

What should I do during a power outage? +

Turn off appliances, check your main switch, and call your power provider if needed.

How do I know if my house needs rewiring? +

Signs include burning smells, frequent trips, old wiring, and warm switches.

Can I do electrical work myself? +

Minor tasks like changing bulbs are fine, but all wiring or repair work must be done by a licensed electrician.
